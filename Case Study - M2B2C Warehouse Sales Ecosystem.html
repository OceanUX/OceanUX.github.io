Designing a M2B2C Warehouse Sales Ecosystem

  • Role: UX/UI Designer
  • Timeline: 6 months
  • Team: 1 PM, 5 Engineers
  • Tools: Sketch, Sprint

Project Overview

A 6-month initiative to design a multi-platform digital ecosystem enabling a manufacturer to run nationwide warehouse sales through its franchise network.

Projects Requirements:

  • A SaaS web platform for manufacturer to manage campaigns and financial auditing
  • An iOS tablet app for franchise staff to issue tickets, place orders, and handle reconciliation
  • A mobile app for end customers to scan event QR codes, place orders, enter lotteries, and track deliveries

Context & Challenge

The manufacturer wanted to digitalise its warehouse sales—traditionally managed with offline tools and spreadsheets.

Challenges:

  • Enabling HQ to set up and manage campaigns while tracking franchise performance.
  • Providing franchisees with a fast, offline-capable system to issue tickets and manage high-volume on-site orders.
  • Giving end customers a smooth way to engage and place orders—despite unstable network conditions in warehouses

My Role & Timeline

I was the sole UX/UI Designer on this project, collaborating with1 project manager and 5 engineers.

  • Led UX strategy and flow design across platforms
  • Created user journeys, wireframes, and high-fidelity mockups in Sketch
  • Worked closely with engineers to design around constraints (like offline handling)
  • Participated in QA and post-launch iterations

Understanding the Users

To design an effective solution, it was essential to understand the distinct needs of manufacturers, franchise staff, and end customers.

User Jounrey Mapping Overview

It is critical that data flows logically and smoothly across the entire system, while each group focuses on their core workflows.

Manufacturer - User Flow

Manufacturers require the ability to easily replicate events for repeated use

Key Decision Points:

Event Definition Validation → Promotion Rule Validation → Card & Coupon Definition Approval → Event Release Readiness → Franchise Assignment Logic → Monitoring Franchise Execution → Post-Event Data Analysis

Manufacturer - Protoype

SaaS System Web

To stay consistent and efficient, we built on the existing back-office UI using familiar patterns like accordion menus, tables, and action button groups.

For complex campaign setups, we visualised activities and branches as a structured flow.

A tab-based layout let users easily switch between branches, making the hierarchy simple to navigate.

Franchise Staff - User Flow

Franchise staff need to manage the end-to-end order process efficiently

Key Decision Points:

Ticket Sales Readiness → Card & Coupon Activation → Order Assistance → After-Sales Adjustment → Delivery Coordination → Reconciliation with manufacture

Franchise Staff - Protoype

Tablet IOS App

The tablet app helped franchise staff assist customers who couldn’t place orders themselves. It focused on quick customer lookup, product selection, and generating payment QR codes.

We used a familiar e-commerce flow with a form-style layout, combining clear steps with buttons and pop-ups to show detailed info without clutter.

End Customer - User Flow

Customers seek a seamless experience to join events and access support.

Key Decision Points:

Join Event and Ticket Activation → Placing an Order → Lottery Participation and Tracking Rewards → After-Sales Support and Order Tracking

End Customer - Protoype

WeChat mobile app

The mobile app enabled a seamless self-service flow by integrating QR scanning and WeChat Pay—allowing customers to add items, enter delivery info, and complete payment in one go.

We focused on clear, timely notifications for lottery results, reward reminders, and after-sales tracking to keep users engaged and informed.

By linking ticket numbers with phone and WeChat accounts, the experience became more personalised while enabling the company to gather richer data for future service improvements.

Launch Learnings

This was the first time the client digitised a warehouse sale, and we didn’t have access to customer testing before the launch.

What Users Said

  • “The system really cut down on manual work—we spent less time fixing errors and more time actually helping customers on-site.”
  • “It felt like everyone got what they needed. We collected better data, dealers had a smoother process, and customers were happy with real discounts.”
  • “The whole event just ran better—it was more organised, and the tech made a big difference.”
  • “The venues were often in remote areas, so the poor signal made it hard for some customers to complete their orders.”

Final Impact & Reflection

This project helped transform a one-off solution into a reusable, scalable product for new B2B2C clients.

Outcomes

  • Real-time sales visibility for HQ for the first time
  • Automated reconciliation between HQ and franchises
  • Two additional clients signed contracts to adopt similar systems

What Can I Improve…

  • SaaS for manufacture: Streamlined branch-level functionality and introduced a modular approach to managing campaign activities.
  • Tablet App for franchise staff: Included offline access to all event-related information to accelerate the order creation process during live sales.
  • WeChat platform for end customer: Simplified the order creation flow and added support for multi-address delivery to enhance user convenience.

If I Had More Time…

  • Conducted in-depth user testing across multiple platforms to gather targeted, actionable feedback.
  • Addressed poor mobile signal conditions by designing solutions aimed at improving customer confidence and engagement.
  • Refined animation interactions and introduced custom interface designs for edge cases, including error states such as 404 and 502 pages
