UK diet trends in 2020
One large trend in the UK is that more people are moving towards eating meat-free and adopting vegetarian, vegan, or pescatarian diets. A recent article from 22 January 2020 highlighted results from an annual survey of 2,000 people in Great Britain looking at UK diet habits, trends, and costs (UK Diet Trends in 2020). Based on this survey, an estimated 12 million Brits intend to be meat-free by the end of 2020, up from the 6.7 million British adults who currently follow a meat-free diet (13%).
Currently millennials are the make up the largest meat-free generation (15% millennials saying they are meat-free), however by the end of 2020, Gen Z will overtake the millennials with 35% with intentions to go meat free compared to 32% of millennials. Of course in reality, intentions don’t always stick and the statistics on intentions will overestimate actual numbers of meat-free eaters at the end 2020. In the 2019 survey, only 5% of those surveyed stuck to their intended diet by the end of 2019.
Comparing the cost of various diets
The type of diet one chooses can also have financial implications. This chart shows the yearly cost of vegan, pescatarian, and vegetarian diets compared to eating a normal balanced diet. Vegetarian diets came in as the most affordable option (1,545 GBP per year) and eating vegan as the most expensive type of diet (2,073 GBP per year).
These figures are based from cost estimates from Tesco Supermarkets (UK Diet Trends in 2020).
Play with seasonal ingredients
Seasonal products ripen perfectly to be both healthy and tasty made by nature with no help. Eating seasonal foods can be healthier as less chemicals and preservatives are used and you can support local farmers and help the environment. It is a natural way to get a diverse intake of vitamins, minerals and other nutrients that our body needs.
Eating seasonally has various health benefits such as: more nutrients, minerals, and trace elements that our bodies need at particular times of the year. For example, butternut squash and apples contain a large amount of vitamin C which can help the body resistance through winter colds. Moreover, it makes economic sense to eat seasonally as fruits and vegetables are sold more cheaply when the supply is abundant.
As it is recommended to eat five portions a day, fruit and vegetables take a stand firm in menu planning. Think about how to pair the seasonal ingredients with other locally produced food sources for a more natural and good tasting meal.
